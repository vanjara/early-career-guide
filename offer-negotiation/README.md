> ⚠️ The most common mistake: accepting on the spot. Companies expect negotiation. Recruiters are not offended by it. The offer is not withdrawn because you asked.

---

## The negotiation starts earlier than you think

Recruiters often ask about salary expectations in the first screening call — before you know the full scope of the role or have any leverage. Your answer there shapes the final offer.

If possible, redirect:

> “I’m still learning about the full scope of the role. What’s the budgeted range for this position?”

If they push for a number, give a range slightly above your minimum. The bottom of your range often becomes the offer.

---

## When you get the call

You do not need to respond immediately. Ever.

> "Thank you so much — I'm really excited about this. Can I have a few days to review everything carefully?"

Standard ask is 3–5 business days. If they pressure you for same-day, that's a signal about the company culture.

---

## Research market rate first

Never negotiate without data. Feelings don't move offers. Numbers do.

| Source | Best for |
|---|---|
| [Levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/) | Tech roles — most accurate, self-reported |
| [Glassdoor](https://www.glassdoor.com/) | Broad — directionally useful |
| [Wall Street Oasis](https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/) | Finance and consulting compensation |
| [LinkedIn Salary](https://www.linkedin.com/salary) | General roles, business tracks |
| [Blind](https://www.teamblind.com/) | Tech — anonymous, candid real numbers |
| [Payscale](https://www.payscale.com/) | Entry-level benchmarking |

## What's actually negotiable

| What | Negotiable? |
|---|---|
| Base salary | Always |
| Signing bonus | Always (often easier to move than base) |
| Start date | Always |
| Annual bonus target | Sometimes |
| Equity / RSUs | Sometimes (especially at startups) |
| Remote/hybrid | Sometimes |
| Title (more flexible at smaller companies — large companies often fix titles to levels) | More than people think |
| Benefits structure, insurance, 401k match | Rarely at big companies |

> 💡 At big companies, base salary has bands — they can move within the band. Signing bonus is often where they have the most flexibility.

---

## How to counter

### For base salary

> "I'm really excited about this role and [Company]. Based on my research on Levels.fyi and conversations with people in similar roles, I was expecting something closer to [X]. Is there flexibility to get there?"

Then stop talking. Let them fill the silence.

### If base won't move — ask about signing bonus

> "I understand if the base is fixed within the band. Would there be flexibility on a signing bonus to bridge the gap?"

### If you have a competing offer

> "I want to be transparent — I have another offer at [X]. [Company] is my first choice, but I want to make sure I'm making the right decision. Is there any room to get closer to that number?"

> ⚠️ A competing offer is the strongest leverage you have. Use it if it's real. Never fabricate one.

## Evaluating the full offer

### Compensation

- Base salary
- Signing bonus (one-time)
- Annual bonus — target % and how often it actually pays out
- Equity / RSUs — total grant, vesting schedule, cliff
### Benefits

- Health insurance — premium and quality
- 401k match and vesting schedule
- PTO — unlimited PTO is often less than a fixed number in practice
- Other: parental leave, FSA/HSA, wellness stipend
### Career

- Learning and development budget
- Promotion timeline — how long do people typically stay at this level?
- Who you'll be working for directly
- Team size and growth trajectory
### Logistics

- Remote / hybrid / in-office
- Start date
- Relocation assistance if needed
> 💡 A $5k higher salary at a company with poor mentorship and no growth path may be worth less than a $5k lower offer where you'll learn faster and get promoted sooner.

## For OPT candidates

> ⚠️ Before accepting any offer:
• Confirm company is enrolled in E-Verify (required for STEM OPT extension)
• Confirm company has sponsored H1B before and will for your role
• Understand your start date relative to the H1B filing timeline

Get these answers before you sign. Not after.

---

## After you accept

- Get the offer letter in writing before giving notice anywhere
- Confirm start date in writing
- Do not post on LinkedIn until you've signed — offers are occasionally rescinded
