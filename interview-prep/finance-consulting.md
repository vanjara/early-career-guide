# Interview Prep — Finance & Consulting Track

## Finance (IB, Corporate Finance, FP&A)

### What to expect

```
Round 1    Recruiter screen — fit and basics
Round 2    First round interviews — behavioral + technical finance
Round 3    Superday — multiple back-to-back interviews, behavioral + technical
```

Superdays are common in IB. You'll meet 4-6 people in one day. Energy and consistency matter as much as answers.

---

### Technical finance — know these cold

These questions come up in almost every finance interview. Know them without hesitation.

**The big three:**

> *"Walk me through a DCF."*

Discount future free cash flows back to present value using WACC. Key inputs: revenue projections, margins, capex, working capital, terminal value, discount rate.

> *"Walk me through the three financial statements."*

Income statement → net income flows to retained earnings on balance sheet and starts cash flow statement. Cash flow statement reconciles net income to actual cash. Balance sheet: assets = liabilities + equity.

> *"What happens to the three statements if depreciation increases by $10?"*

Income statement: EBIT down $10, tax down ~$3 (assuming 30% tax rate), net income down ~$7. Cash flow: net income down $7, add back depreciation +$10, cash from operations up $3. Balance sheet: PP&E down $10, cash up $3, retained earnings down $7. Assets down $7, equity down $7. Balanced.

**Other questions to prepare:**

- What is EBITDA and why do analysts use it?
- What is enterprise value vs. equity value?
- Walk me through an LBO at a high level
- What makes a good LBO candidate?
- How do you value a company? (DCF, comparable companies, precedent transactions)
- When would you use one valuation method over another?
- What is beta? What does a beta > 1 mean?
- Why can two companies with the same revenue have very different valuations?

### Behavioral for finance

Finance interviews weight fit heavily — especially in IB where you're working with the same team for long hours.

Common questions:
> *"Why investment banking / finance?"*
> *"Why this firm specifically?"*
> *"Walk me through your resume."*
> *"Tell me about a time you worked under pressure."*
> *"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"*

**"Why this firm?"** — do real research. Know recent deals they've done, their industry focus, something specific about their culture. Generic answers ("great culture, strong deal flow") don't work.

## Consulting

### What to expect

```
Round 1    First round — 2 case interviews
Round 2    Final round — 2-3 case interviews + fit
```

Cases are the filter. Behavioral matters but cases are what most people fail.

---

### Case interviews

A case is a business problem you solve out loud with the interviewer. They're testing:
- Structured thinking — can you break a problem into parts?
- Business judgment — do your recommendations make sense?
- Communication — can you lead the conversation?
- Math — can you do back-of-envelope calculations quickly?

**Structure for every case:**

```
1. Clarify         Ask 1-2 clarifying questions before structuring
2. Structure       Lay out your framework out loud before diving in
3. Analyze         Work through each area, prioritize the most important
4. Synthesize      "Based on what we've discussed, my recommendation is..."
```

**Common case types:**
- Profitability (why are profits declining?)
- Market entry (should we enter this market?)
- Pricing (how should we price this product?)
- M&A (should we acquire this company?)
- Operations (how do we reduce costs?)

---

### Case prep resources

| Resource | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Case in Point** (book) | The standard reference — read it first |
| **IGotAnOffer** | Structured case practice, good frameworks |
| **MConsultingPrep** | Video walkthroughs of full cases |
| **Firm websites** (McKinsey, BCG, Bain) | Practice cases published by the firms themselves |

**Non-negotiable:** Practice cases out loud with a partner. Solo reading is not enough. You need reps talking through cases in real time. Find a partner doing the same prep and practice 3-4x per week.

---

### Math for cases

You'll be asked to do mental math in cases. Practice:
- Percentages and percentage changes
- Market sizing (population × segment × penetration × price)
- Break-even analysis
- Profit = Revenue − Cost breakdowns

You don't need to be fast. You need to be accurate and show your work out loud.

---

### Fit for consulting

> *"Tell me about yourself."*
> *"Why consulting?"*
> *"Why this firm?"*
> *"Give me an example of a time you led a team through ambiguity."*
> *"Tell me about a time you had to persuade someone who disagreed with you."*

Consulting firms care a lot about leadership and impact stories. Use the STAR method. Have 5-6 stories that you can adapt to different questions.
