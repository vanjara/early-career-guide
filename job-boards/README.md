# Job Boards

## Understand the channels first

Jobs get filled through 4 channels, roughly in order of effectiveness:

```
1. Referrals          — someone vouches for you internally
2. Direct apply       — company career page, targeted
3. Campus recruiting  — structured programs for new grads
4. Job boards         — highest volume, lowest conversion
```

Most people start at #4. This hub pushes you toward #1 and #3.

## OPT / Visa candidates

| Platform | Best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **myvisajobs.com** | OPT/H1B | Filters roles by H1B sponsoring employers |
| **H1Bdata.info** | Research | USCIS public data — see exactly which companies filed, how many, at what salaries. Use this to vet companies before applying |
| **Immigrationhelp.org** | OPT/visa | Visa-specific job board |
| **LinkedIn** (sponsor filter) | All tracks | Search "visa sponsorship" in filters — not perfect but helps narrow |

**Important:** Before applying anywhere, check the company on H1Bdata.info. A company saying "we consider sponsorship" means nothing. Actual filing history does.

## Direct company applications — don't skip this

Job boards are where candidates go. Company career pages are where jobs actually get filled.

Build a list of 20-30 companies you want to work at. Bookmark their careers page. Check weekly.

### Structured new grad programs

These have specific application windows — usually August–October:

**Tech:**
- Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Apple — dedicated new grad hiring cycles
- Apply directly on their career pages, not through job boards

**Finance / Consulting:**
- Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley — summer analyst applications open August
- McKinsey, BCG, Bain — recruiting starts September–October for following year
- Big 4 (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) — campus recruiting calendar on their websites

**If you're a May grad:** Structured programs are mostly closed by the time you graduate. Apply to the next cycle early, and focus energy on direct applications + networking for immediate roles.

## The rule on job boards

Pick 2-3 maximum. Work them well.

```
New grad, any track:     Handshake + track-specific board + company career pages
Tech:                    Handshake + Wellfound + Levels.fyi
Finance:                 Handshake + eFinancialCareers + WSO
Data Analytics:          Handshake + Simplify.jobs + Built In
OPT candidates:          All of the above + myvisajobs.com + H1Bdata.info for vetting
```

More boards = more noise. Fewer boards, better applications, more outreach = better results.
