# Networking

> Networking for job search is simple: talk to people who are doing what you want to do, and learn from them. You're not asking for favors. Most people are happy to help because someone helped them.

---

## The three sources of people to talk to

| Source | Difficulty | Response rate |
|---|---|---|
| Alumni from your university | Easiest | Highest |
| People you've worked with before | Easy | High |
| Cold outreach to strangers | Harder | Lower — but scales |

Start with alumni and former colleagues. Don't start with cold outreach.

---

## Alumni network — step by step

**How to find them on LinkedIn:**
1. Go to your university's LinkedIn page
2. Click "Alumni"
3. Filter by: Where they work, What they do, When they graduated
4. Look for people 2–5 years ahead of you

**✅ Alumni outreach message:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm a recent [University] grad in [field] and came across your profile. I'm exploring roles in [area] and noticed you've been at [Company] for a couple of years — I'd love to hear how you got there and what the work is actually like. Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call sometime this week or next?"

What makes this work:
- You're not asking for a job or a referral
- You have a specific reason for reaching out
- The ask is small (20 minutes)

💡 Expect 20–30% response rate. If 10 people don't respond, 2–3 will. That's 2–3 conversations you wouldn't have had.

---

## The informational interview

A 20–30 minute call where you ask someone about their experience. Not a job interview — a conversation.

**What to ask:**

Opening:
- "How did you end up at [Company] / in [field]?"
- "What does a typical week look like for you?"

The real stuff:
- "What do you wish you'd known when you were job searching?"
- "What do companies in this space actually look for in new grads?"
- "Is there anyone else you'd recommend I talk to?"

The close:
- "Is there anything I could send you — resume, portfolio — where you could give me feedback?"

💡 Always ask the "anyone else" question. Five conversations can turn into fifteen through introductions.

**✅ Thank you message (send within 24 hours):**
> "Thanks so much for your time today — the advice on [specific thing] was really helpful. I'll keep you posted on how the search goes."

## Cold outreach on LinkedIn

**Hooks that get responses:**
- You read something they wrote (post, article)
- You're applying to their company and want to learn more first
- You share a specific background (same city, same major, similar path)

**✅ Cold outreach template:**
> "Hi [Name], I've been following your posts on [topic] and found your take on [specific thing] really useful. I'm a recent grad exploring roles in [area] and your path from [X to Y] caught my attention. Would you be open to a 20-minute call? Happy to work around your schedule."

**❌ What kills cold outreach:**
- Generic openers: "I am very passionate about your company"
- Attaching your resume in the first message
- Asking for a referral before having a conversation
- Copy-pasting the same message to everyone — people can tell

## LinkedIn profile — the other side of networking

**Headline:**
- ❌ "Computer Science Graduate | Looking for Full-Time Opportunities"
- ✅ "Data Analyst | Python, SQL, Tableau | Recent grad @ [University]"

**About section (3–4 sentences):**
> "I'm a recent Business Analytics grad from [University] with a focus on financial modeling and data visualization. I've worked on [X] and [Y] and I'm looking for analyst roles where I can work with messy data and turn it into decisions. Always open to conversations about data, finance, or [relevant interest]."

**Experience:** Same rules as resume — action verbs, results, numbers.

## Your weekly networking system

```
Weekly routine:
→ 5 new outreach messages sent
→ 1–2 calls or conversations
→ Follow up on anyone you spoke to 1–2 weeks ago
→ Connect with anyone you met (same day)

Track in a spreadsheet:
Name | Company | How connected | Date reached | Status | Notes
```

## All outreach templates

**Alumni outreach:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm a recent [University] grad in [field] and came across your profile. I'm exploring roles in [area] and noticed you've been at [Company] for a couple of years — I'd love to hear how you got there and what the work is actually like. Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call sometime this week or next?"

**Cold outreach — general:**
> "Hi [Name], I've been following your posts on [topic] and found your take on [specific thing] really useful. I'm a recent grad exploring roles in [area] and your path from [X to Y] caught my attention. Would you be open to a 20-minute call? Happy to work around your schedule."

**Outreach before applying:**
> "Hi [Name], I came across the [Role] posting at [Company] and it lines up closely with my background in [X]. Before applying I wanted to get a sense of the team from someone inside. Would you have 15–20 minutes to chat? No pressure at all."

**Referral ask (after a conversation):**
> "Thanks again for the chat last week — it was really helpful. I'm planning to apply for the [Role] at [Company]. Would you be comfortable putting in a referral, or is there someone you'd suggest I reach out to?"

**Hiring manager outreach:**
> "Hi [Name], I came across the [Role] posting at [Company] and it lines up closely with my background in [X]. I'd love to learn more about what you're looking for — would you be open to a quick 15-minute call?"

**Follow-up if no response (once, after 1 week):**
> "Hi [Name], just following up on my message from last week — happy to work around your schedule if timing was the issue. Either way, appreciate your time."
