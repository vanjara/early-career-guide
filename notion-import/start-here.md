# Early Career Job Search Guide

> No hustle content. No paid courses. Just a practical system — what to do, in what order, with templates you can use today.

---

## Who this is for

- Fresh graduates in tech — SWE, data engineering, data science, data analytics
- Fresh graduates in business and finance — analysts, consulting, corporate finance
- Candidates on F-1 OPT or student visa navigating job search with visa constraints
- Master's students (MS in CS, data science, analytics, finance) transitioning or pivoting

**If you're an MS student:** The positioning question matters more than the degree itself. Target roles that explicitly value a graduate degree — don't apply as if you're competing with undergrads. Your thesis or capstone project, TA or research experience, and any pre-MS work experience are all legitimate portfolio and resume items — lead with them.

---

## Where are you right now?

Pick your situation and go straight to what you need.

**🟢 I haven't started yet**

Start here, in this order:
1. Fix your resume first — nothing else matters if your resume isn't solid
2. Set up your LinkedIn profile
3. Build your target company list (20–30 companies)
4. Start applying directly + start outreach in parallel

**🟡 I'm applying but not hearing back**

Your problem is likely one of three things:
- Resume isn't passing ATS → go to Resume
- Applying through Easy Apply → go to Job Search Strategy
- Applying to the wrong places → go to Job Boards

The fix is almost never "apply to more jobs." It's apply differently.

**🟠 I'm getting interviews but not converting**

You've cleared the resume and application stage — that's more than most people. Now it's about prep:
- Behavioral interviews → Interview Prep → Behavioral section
- Technical screen → Interview Prep → your track
- You freeze up or go blank → do mock interviews, not more reading

**🔵 I have an offer — now what?**

Don't accept on the spot. Go to Offer & Negotiation + Benefits.

## The honest timeline

> Actively searching, doing everything right: 8–12 weeks to an offer
> Passively applying, Easy Apply only: 5–6 months, maybe longer
> OPT candidates: Start before graduation — your clock starts at graduation day
>
> If you're 3 weeks in and discouraged — that's normal.
> If you're 3 months in with no traction — something in your system needs to change, not just your effort level.
