[H1] Interview Prep

[CALLOUT] 💡
Knowing the structure removes the anxiety of not knowing what's coming. Most companies run the same 4–5 step process.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] How hiring interviews actually work

[TABLE]
Round | What it is | What they're checking
Recruiter screen | 15–20 min phone call | Fit, basics, salary expectations
Hiring manager screen | 30 min | Your background and genuine interest
Technical / case round | 45–60 min | Role-specific skills (see tracks below)
Final round / onsite | 3–5 back-to-back interviews | Deep fit + technical
Offer | — | —

Not every company runs all five. Startups often compress to 2–3 rounds. The recruiter screen is eliminatory — clear it and you're in the real process.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] The recruiter screen

They're checking three things:
[BULLET] Can you talk about yourself clearly?
[BULLET] Do your background and expectations match the role?
[BULLET] Are you a reasonable human being?

Questions you'll always get:

[TOGGLE] "Tell me about yourself" — 90-second answer
Structure:
→ Present: who you are and what you do
→ Past: relevant experience that got you here
→ Future: what you're looking for and why this role

Example: "I'm a recent Data Analytics grad from [University] where I focused on SQL and Python for business problems. During my internship at [Company] I built dashboards the sales team used daily to track pipeline. I'm now looking for an analyst role where I can work on real business problems with data — which is why this role caught my attention."

[TOGGLE] "What are your salary expectations?"
Never give a number first if you can avoid it.

→ "I'm still learning about the full scope of the role. What's the budgeted range for this position?"

If they push: give a range based on Levels.fyi (tech) or Glassdoor/WSO (finance). Anchor slightly high.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Behavioral interviews — every role, every company

[H3] The STAR method

[TABLE]
Letter | What it means | Tips
S — Situation | Set the context briefly | 1–2 sentences max
T — Task | What were you responsible for | Be specific about your role
A — Action | What YOU specifically did | Not "we" — what did you do
R — Result | What happened | Quantify if possible

[H3] Common questions and what they're really asking

[TABLE]
Question | What they want to see
"Tell me about a time you faced a challenge" | Resilience, problem-solving
"Tell me about a time you worked with a difficult person" | Maturity, communication
"Tell me about a project you're proud of" | Ownership, impact
"Tell me about a time you failed" | Self-awareness, learning
"Tell me about a time you led something" | Initiative, leadership

[CALLOUT] 💡
Prepare 5–6 strong stories from your experience. Internships, class projects, part-time jobs, student orgs all count. Each story should be flexible enough to answer 2–3 different questions. One strong story beats ten weak ones.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Technical prep — by track

[TOGGLE] 💻 Tech (SWE, Data Engineering, Data Science)
LeetCode-style coding problems, 1–2 per round, 45 minutes, talking through your thinking out loud.

Focus areas:
[TABLE]
Priority | Topics
Must know | Arrays, strings, hashmaps, two pointers, sliding window
Should know | Trees, graphs, BFS/DFS, recursion
Good to have | Dynamic programming, heaps, tries

Resources:
[TABLE]
Resource | What it's for
Neetcode.io | Structured roadmap, video explanations — best starting point
LeetCode | Do Neetcode 150, not random problems
interviewing.io | Mock interviews with real engineers

During the interview:
[NUMBERED] Clarify before coding — ask about constraints and edge cases
[NUMBERED] Talk through your approach before writing a line of code
[NUMBERED] Start with brute force, then optimize
[NUMBERED] Test your code with an example before saying you're done
[NUMBERED] Keep talking — silence is worse than thinking out loud wrong

[TOGGLE] 📊 Data Analytics
Expect SQL + take-home case + behavioral.

SQL (know cold):
[BULLET] JOINs, GROUP BY, HAVING, WHERE
[BULLET] Window functions — ROW_NUMBER, RANK, LAG, LEAD, SUM OVER
[BULLET] CTEs and subqueries
[BULLET] CASE WHEN, date functions

Practice resources: DataLemur, StrataScratch (real questions from Meta, Amazon, Google)

Statistics to know:
[BULLET] A/B testing — what it is, how to interpret results
[BULLET] p-value — in plain English, not textbook definition
[BULLET] Statistical vs. practical significance
[BULLET] Correlation vs. causation

Take-home case structure:
[NUMBERED] What question are you answering?
[NUMBERED] What did you find? (key insight first, not methodology first)
[NUMBERED] What does it mean for the business?
[NUMBERED] What would you recommend?
[NUMBERED] What are the limitations?

[TOGGLE] 💰 Finance & Consulting
Finance — technical questions to know cold:

[TABLE]
Question | Key points
Walk me through a DCF | Future free cash flows → discount at WACC → terminal value
Three financial statements | Net income flows to retained earnings + starts cash flow statement
Depreciation increases $10 | Net income -$7, cash from ops +$3, PP&E -$10, balanced
What is EBITDA? | Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization
Enterprise vs equity value | EV = equity + debt - cash

Resources: Mergers & Inquisitions (free), Wall Street Prep, Breaking Into Wall Street

Consulting — case interviews:
[NUMBERED] Clarify — ask 1–2 questions before structuring
[NUMBERED] Structure — lay out your framework out loud
[NUMBERED] Analyze — work through each area, prioritize what matters most
[NUMBERED] Synthesize — "My recommendation is..."

Practice: Case in Point (book), IGotAnOffer, firm-published practice cases (McKinsey, BCG, Bain)

Non-negotiable: practice cases out loud with a partner. Solo reading is not enough.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Questions to ask the interviewer

Always prepare 2–3 questions. "I don't have any questions" is a red flag.

[TOGGLE] Good questions to ask
[BULLET] "What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?"
[BULLET] "What's the biggest challenge the team is working through right now?"
[BULLET] "What do you like most about working here?"
[BULLET] "What are the growth paths from this role?"

Avoid asking about salary, vacation, or remote work in early rounds. There's a time for that — it's not the first interview.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] After the interview

[CALLOUT] 💡
Same day: Send a thank you email. Two sentences.

"Thanks for taking the time today — I really enjoyed learning about [specific thing discussed]. It's made me even more excited about the role."

If you don't hear back after the timeline they gave you — one follow-up:
"Hi [Name], just wanted to check in on the [Role] — happy to provide anything additional if helpful."

One follow-up. Not three.

[H2] Prep timeline

[TABLE]
When | What to do
4+ weeks out | Build story bank (5–6 STAR stories). Start technical prep.
2–3 weeks out | Mock interviews with a friend or recorded solo. Research target companies.
1 week out | Review stories out loud. Research specific interviewers on LinkedIn.
Day before | Light review, sleep well. Prep your setup if virtual.
