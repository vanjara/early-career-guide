[H1] Networking

[CALLOUT] 💡
Networking for job search is simple: talk to people who are doing what you want to do, and learn from them. You're not asking for favors. You're having conversations with people who were in your exact position not long ago — and most of them are happy to help.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] The three sources of people to talk to

[TABLE]
Source | Difficulty | Response rate
Alumni from your university | Easiest | Highest — you share something real
People you've worked with before | Easy | High — existing relationship
Cold outreach to strangers | Harder | Lower — but scales

Start with alumni and former colleagues. Don't start with cold outreach.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Alumni network — step by step

The most underused resource for new grads. Alumni respond because you share the same school, professors, and probably similar experiences.

[H3] How to find them on LinkedIn
[NUMBERED] Go to your university's LinkedIn page
[NUMBERED] Click "Alumni"
[NUMBERED] Filter by: Where they work, What they do, When they graduated
[NUMBERED] Look for people 2–5 years ahead of you — recent enough to remember the struggle, established enough to have influence

[TOGGLE] ✅ Alumni outreach message
"Hi [Name], I'm a recent [University] grad in [field] and came across your profile. I'm exploring roles in [area] and noticed you've been at [Company] for a couple of years — I'd love to hear how you got there and what the work is actually like. Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call sometime this week or next?"

What makes this work:
[BULLET] You're not asking for a job or a referral
[BULLET] You have a specific reason for reaching out (same school, their role)
[BULLET] The ask is small (20 minutes)

[CALLOUT] 💡 Expect 20–30% response rate on good outreach. If 10 people don't respond, 2–3 will. That's 2–3 conversations you wouldn't have had.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] The informational interview

A 20–30 minute call where you ask someone about their experience. Not a job interview — a conversation.

[TOGGLE] What to ask

Opening
[BULLET] "How did you end up at [Company] / in [field]?"
[BULLET] "What does a typical week look like for you?"

The real stuff
[BULLET] "What do you wish you'd known when you were job searching?"
[BULLET] "What do companies in this space actually look for in new grads?"
[BULLET] "Is there anyone else you'd recommend I talk to?"

The close
[BULLET] "Is there anything I could send you — resume, portfolio — where you could give me feedback?"

[CALLOUT] 💡 Always ask the "anyone else" question. Five conversations can turn into fifteen through introductions alone.

[TOGGLE] ✅ Thank you message (send within 24 hours)
"Thanks so much for your time today — the advice on [specific thing they said] was really helpful. I'll keep you posted on how the search goes."

Then actually keep them posted when something happens. People like knowing they helped.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Cold outreach on LinkedIn

Once you've exhausted alumni and warm connections, go broader.

[H3] Hooks that get responses
[BULLET] You read something they wrote (post, article)
[BULLET] You're applying to their company and want to learn more first
[BULLET] You share a specific background (same city, same major, similar career path)

[TOGGLE] ✅ Cold outreach template
"Hi [Name], I've been following your posts on [topic] and found your take on [specific thing] really useful. I'm a recent grad exploring roles in [area] and your path from [X to Y] caught my attention. Would you be open to a 20-minute call? Happy to work around your schedule."

[TOGGLE] ❌ What kills cold outreach
[BULLET] Generic openers: "I am very passionate about your company"
[BULLET] Attaching your resume in the first message
[BULLET] Asking for a referral before having a conversation
[BULLET] Copy-pasting the same message to 50 people — people can tell

[DIVIDER]

[H2] LinkedIn profile — the other side of networking

When people receive your outreach, they look at your profile immediately. It needs to hold up.

[TOGGLE] Headline — don't just put your degree
❌ "Computer Science Graduate | Looking for Full-Time Opportunities"
✅ "Data Analyst | Python, SQL, Tableau | Recent grad @ [University]"

[TOGGLE] About section — 3–4 sentences, written like a human
"I'm a recent Business Analytics grad from [University] with a focus on financial modeling and data visualization. I've worked on [X] and [Y] and I'm looking for analyst roles where I can work with messy data and turn it into decisions. Always open to conversations about data, finance, or [relevant interest]."

[TOGGLE] Experience
Same rules as resume — action verbs, results, numbers.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Your weekly networking system

[QUOTE]
Weekly routine:
→ 5 new outreach messages sent
→ 1–2 calls or conversations
→ Follow up on anyone you spoke to 1–2 weeks ago
→ Connect with anyone you met or spoke to (same day)

Track it in a simple spreadsheet:
Name | Company | How connected | Date reached | Status | Notes

[H2] Outreach templates

[TOGGLE] Alumni outreach
"Hi [Name], I'm a recent [University] grad in [field] and came across your profile. I'm exploring roles in [area] and noticed you've been at [Company] for a couple of years — I'd love to hear how you got there and what the work is actually like. Would you have 20 minutes for a quick call sometime this week or next?"

[TOGGLE] Cold outreach — general
"Hi [Name], I've been following your posts on [topic] and found your take on [specific thing] really useful. I'm a recent grad exploring roles in [area] and your path from [X to Y] caught my attention. Would you be open to a 20-minute call? Happy to work around your schedule."

[TOGGLE] Outreach before applying to a company
"Hi [Name], I came across the [Role] posting at [Company] and it lines up closely with my background in [X]. Before applying I wanted to get a sense of the team and culture from someone inside. Would you have 15–20 minutes to chat? No pressure at all."

[TOGGLE] Referral ask (after a conversation)
"Thanks again for the chat last week — it was really helpful. I'm planning to apply for the [Role] at [Company]. Would you be comfortable putting in a referral, or is there someone specific you'd suggest I reach out to on the team?"

[TOGGLE] Hiring manager outreach (startups / smaller companies)
"Hi [Name], I came across the [Role] posting at [Company] and it lines up closely with my background in [X]. I'd love to learn more about the team and what you're looking for — would you be open to a quick 15-minute call?"

[TOGGLE] Follow-up if no response (send once, after 1 week)
"Hi [Name], just following up on my message from last week — happy to work around your schedule if timing was the issue. Either way, appreciate your time."

[CALLOUT] 💡
Personalize every message. Reference something specific. Keep it short. One clear ask. Don't follow up more than once on cold outreach.
