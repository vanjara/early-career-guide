[H1] Visa / OPT Track

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
Read this before you start applying. If you're on F-1 OPT, your job search has hard constraints that don't apply to other candidates. Ignoring them doesn't make them go away.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] The OPT timeline — what you can't miss

[TABLE]
When | What to do
60 days before graduation | Apply for OPT through your DSO. USCIS processing takes 3–5 months — apply early.
Graduation | OPT start date can be up to 60 days after graduation. Choose your start date strategically.
OPT active | 12 months to work. 90-day unemployment limit — days without a job count against you.
Before OPT expires | Employer files H1B (April lottery, starts October). STEM extension buys more time if not selected.

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
The most common mistake: waiting until graduation to start the job search. By then you may have already burned 60 days of your unemployment clock. Start before you graduate.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] STEM OPT extension

If your degree is in a STEM field (most CS, data, engineering, many business analytics programs), you're eligible for a 24-month extension — giving you 36 months total.

Requirements:
[BULLET] Degree must be on the STEM designated degree program list (check uscis.gov)
[BULLET] Employer must be enrolled in E-Verify
[BULLET] Must apply before your initial OPT expires

[CALLOUT] 💡
STEM OPT gives you two more H1B lottery cycles. That dramatically improves your odds over time. Confirm whether your degree qualifies — your DSO can tell you.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] The 90-day unemployment rule

During OPT you can only be unemployed for 90 days total (150 days with STEM extension). Days accumulate whether you're tracking them or not.

[TOGGLE] What counts as employment
✅ Full-time job
✅ Part-time job (must be related to your degree)
✅ Contract / freelance work (if structured correctly — talk to your DSO)
❌ Volunteer work
❌ Unpaid internships
❌ Job searching

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
Track your days. Your DSO tracks them too. If you're approaching 60 days unemployed, escalate your urgency.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Finding companies that actually sponsor

[CALLOUT] 💡
"We consider all candidates" often means "we don't sponsor." The only thing that matters is whether a company has actually filed H1B petitions before — and you can look this up.

[H3] How to vet a company — step by step

[NUMBERED] Go to H1Bdata.info
[NUMBERED] Search the company name
[NUMBERED] Look at: petitions filed (last 3 years), approval rate, job titles sponsored, salary ranges

[TABLE]
Signal | What it means
100+ petitions, 90%+ approval rate | Established sponsor, real process
10–50 petitions, good approval rate | Sponsors selectively — ask about process early
1–5 petitions total | Case-by-case — high risk
Zero petitions | Almost certainly won't sponsor despite what they say

[TOGGLE] General patterns (not guarantees — always verify)
✅ Typically sponsor: Large tech (FAANG, Microsoft), Big 4 consulting, major banks, large healthcare/pharma
⚠️ Varies: Startups, mid-size tech, consulting boutiques
❌ Rarely/never: Government contractors, small businesses, most retail/hospitality

[DIVIDER]

[H2] When to bring up visa status

Bring it up before the final round — not at the offer stage.

Raising it after 4 rounds of interviews wastes everyone's time and burns goodwill.

Best time: the recruiter screen, or right after you pass it.

[QUOTE]
"I want to be transparent — I'm currently on F-1 OPT and will need H1B sponsorship down the line. I wanted to make sure that's something [Company] is able to support before we go further."

[DIVIDER]

[H2] The H1B lottery

Your employer files an H1B petition on your behalf in April. USCIS runs a lottery.

[TABLE]
Date | What happens
April 1 | Registration window opens
Mid-April | Lottery results announced
April–June | Selected employers file full petitions
October 1 | H1B status begins (new fiscal year)

Cap numbers (approximate):
[BULLET] 65,000 regular cap
[BULLET] 20,000 additional for US master's degree holders — if you have a US master's, you get two lottery entries

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
Lottery odds have been roughly 30–40% in recent years. Even doing everything right, there's a real chance of not being selected the first time. Have a backup plan before April results come out.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] CPT — if you're still in school

[TOGGLE] What is CPT and what to watch out for
Curricular Practical Training lets you work while enrolled.

✅ Must be authorized by your DSO
✅ Must be directly related to your degree
⚠️ Full-time CPT for 12+ months eliminates your OPT eligibility
✅ Part-time CPT does not affect OPT

If you're using CPT for an internship — confirm it doesn't accidentally eat your OPT. Talk to your DSO before accepting any CPT authorization.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] If your job search is running long

[TOGGLE] Short term options
- Freelance / contract work — counts as employment if structured correctly, talk to your DSO
- Part-time role — buys time while you search full-time
- On-campus employment — limited but an option if still connected to university

[TOGGLE] Medium term options
- STEM OPT extension — apply before your OPT expires if eligible
- Graduate school — resets your F-1 clock, gives you another OPT cycle
- Change of status — talk to an immigration attorney, not Reddit

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
If your OPT expires and you haven't filed for an extension or changed status, you fall out of status immediately. There is no grace period. Talk to your DSO well before that date.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] OPT job search checklist

[TOGGLE] ☐ Before graduation
☐ OPT application submitted to DSO (60+ days before graduation)
☐ Confirmed whether your degree is STEM-designated
☐ Job search actively started

[TOGGLE] ☐ Once OPT begins
☐ Tracking unemployment days
☐ Every company checked on H1Bdata.info before applying
☐ Visa status raised in recruiter screen, not at offer stage

[TOGGLE] ☐ At offer stage
☐ Confirmed company is E-Verify enrolled (required for STEM extension)
☐ Sponsorship commitment discussed with HR
☐ Understand start date relative to H1B filing timeline

[TOGGLE] ☐ H1B cycle
☐ Employer registers by April 1
☐ Know lottery result before making other plans
☐ Have a backup plan if not selected
