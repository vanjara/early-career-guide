[H1] Offer & Negotiation

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
The most common mistake: accepting on the spot. Companies expect negotiation. Recruiters are not offended by it. The offer is not withdrawn because you asked.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] When you get the call

You do not need to respond immediately. Ever.

[QUOTE]
"Thank you so much — I'm really excited about this. Can I have a few days to review everything carefully?"

Standard ask is 3–5 business days. Most companies give it without question. If they pressure you for same-day, that's a signal about the company culture.

Use those days to:
[NUMBERED] Research market rate for the role
[NUMBERED] Evaluate the full package — not just base salary
[NUMBERED] Decide what you actually want to ask for

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Research market rate first

Never negotiate without data. Feelings don't move offers. Numbers do.

[TABLE]
Source | Best for
Levels.fyi | Tech roles — most accurate, self-reported
Glassdoor | Broad — directionally useful
Wall Street Oasis | Finance and consulting compensation
LinkedIn Salary | General roles, business tracks
Blind | Tech — anonymous, candid real numbers
Payscale | Entry-level benchmarking

[DIVIDER]

[H2] What's actually negotiable

[TABLE]
What | Negotiable?
Base salary | Always
Signing bonus | Always (often easier to move than base)
Start date | Always
Annual bonus target | Sometimes
Equity / RSUs | Sometimes (especially at startups)
Remote/hybrid | Sometimes
Title | More than people think
Benefits structure, insurance, 401k match | Rarely at big companies

[CALLOUT] 💡
At big companies, base salary has bands — they can move within the band. Signing bonus is often where they have the most flexibility.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] How to counter

Give a number and a reason. Don't just say "can you do better."

[TOGGLE] For base salary
"I'm really excited about this role and [Company]. Based on my research on Levels.fyi and conversations with people in similar roles, I was expecting something closer to [X]. Is there flexibility to get there?"

Then stop talking. Let them fill the silence.

[TOGGLE] If base won't move — ask about signing bonus
"I understand if the base is fixed within the band. Would there be flexibility on a signing bonus to bridge the gap?"

[TOGGLE] If you have a competing offer
"I want to be transparent — I have another offer at [X]. [Company] is my first choice, but I want to make sure I'm making the right decision. Is there any room to get closer to that number?"

[CALLOUT] ⚠️ A competing offer is the strongest leverage you have. Use it if it's real. Never fabricate one.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] Evaluating the full offer

[TOGGLE] Compensation
[BULLET] Base salary
[BULLET] Signing bonus (one-time)
[BULLET] Annual bonus — target % and how often it actually pays out
[BULLET] Equity / RSUs — total grant, vesting schedule, cliff

[TOGGLE] Benefits
[BULLET] Health insurance — premium and quality (see Benefits section)
[BULLET] 401k match and vesting schedule
[BULLET] PTO — unlimited PTO is often less than a fixed number in practice
[BULLET] Other: parental leave, FSA/HSA, wellness stipend

[TOGGLE] Career
[BULLET] Learning and development budget
[BULLET] Promotion timeline — how long do people typically stay at this level?
[BULLET] Who you'll be working for directly
[BULLET] Team size and growth trajectory

[TOGGLE] Logistics
[BULLET] Remote / hybrid / in-office
[BULLET] Start date
[BULLET] Relocation assistance if needed

[CALLOUT] 💡
A $5k higher salary at a company with poor mentorship and no growth path may be worth less than a $5k lower offer where you'll learn faster and get promoted sooner.

[H2] For OPT candidates

[CALLOUT] ⚠️
Before accepting any offer, confirm:
[BULLET] Company is enrolled in E-Verify (required for STEM OPT extension)
[BULLET] Company has sponsored H1B before — and will for your role
[BULLET] You understand your start date relative to the H1B filing timeline

Get these answers before you sign. Not after.

[DIVIDER]

[H2] After you accept

[BULLET] Get the offer letter in writing before giving notice anywhere
[BULLET] Confirm start date in writing
[BULLET] Do not post on LinkedIn until you've signed — offers are occasionally rescinded

[DIVIDER]

[H2] If the offer is rescinded

Rare but happens, especially in volatile markets (tech layoffs, banking freezes).

[BULLET] Get documentation that the offer was made and rescinded
[BULLET] Talk to your university career center — they sometimes have company relationships and can advocate
[BULLET] OPT candidates: talk to your DSO immediately — the clock implications are real
[BULLET] Some states have legal protections for rescinded offers — worth knowing
