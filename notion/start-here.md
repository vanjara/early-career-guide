[H2] Where are you right now?

Pick your situation and go straight to what you need.

[CALLOUT] 🟢 I haven't started yet
Start here, in this order:
1. Fix your resume first — nothing else matters if your resume isn't solid
2. Set up your LinkedIn profile
3. Build your target company list (20–30 companies)
4. Start applying directly + start outreach in parallel

→ Go to: Resume

[CALLOUT] 🟡 I'm applying but not hearing back
Your problem is likely one of three things:
- Resume isn't passing ATS → go to Resume
- Applying through Easy Apply → go to Job Search Strategy
- Applying to the wrong places → go to Job Boards

The fix is almost never "apply to more jobs." It's apply differently.

→ Go to: Job Search Strategy

[CALLOUT] 🟠 I'm getting interviews but not converting
You've cleared the resume and application stage — that's already more than most people. Now it's about prep.
- Behavioral interviews → Interview Prep → Behavioral section
- Technical screen → Interview Prep → your track
- You freeze up or go blank → do mock interviews, not more reading

→ Go to: Interview Prep

[CALLOUT] 🔵 I have an offer — now what?
Don't accept on the spot. Read this first.

→ Go to: Offer & Negotiation + Benefits

[H2] The honest timeline

[QUOTE]
Actively searching, doing everything right: 8–12 weeks to an offer
Passively applying, Easy Apply only: 5–6 months, maybe longer
OPT candidates: Start before graduation — your clock starts at graduation day

If you're 3 weeks in and discouraged — that's normal.
If you're 3 months in with no traction — something in your system needs to change, not just your effort level.
