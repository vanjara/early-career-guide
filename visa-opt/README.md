> ⚠️ Read this before you start applying. If you're on F-1 OPT, your job search has hard constraints that don't apply to other candidates. Ignoring them doesn't make them go away.

Your **DSO (Designated School Official)** is the advisor at your university’s international student office who manages your F-1 status. They authorize OPT, CPT, and STEM extensions. When this guide says “talk to your DSO” — this is the person.

---

## The OPT timeline — what you can't miss

| When | What to do |
|---|---|
| 60–90 days before graduation | Apply for OPT through your DSO. USCIS processing takes 3–5 months — apply early. |
| Graduation | OPT start date can be up to 60 days after graduation. Choose your start date strategically. |
| OPT active | 12 months to work. 90-day unemployment limit — days without a job count against you. |
| Before OPT expires | Employer files H1B (April lottery, starts October). STEM extension buys more time if not selected. |

> ⚠️ The most common mistake: waiting until graduation to start the job search. By then you may have already burned 60 days of your unemployment clock. Start before you graduate.

> 📅 On your OPT start date: Your unemployment clock starts on your OPT start date — not graduation. If you graduate May 15 and set your OPT start date to July 1, your clock doesn’t begin until July 1. Choose your start date strategically. Students who start OPT immediately sometimes burn unemployment days before their EAD card even arrives.

## STEM OPT extension

If your degree is in a STEM field, you're eligible for a 24-month extension — giving you 36 months total.

Requirements:

- Degree must be on the STEM designated degree program list (check uscis.gov)
- Employer must be enrolled in E-Verify
- Must apply before your initial OPT expires
> 💡 STEM OPT gives you two more H1B lottery cycles. That dramatically improves your odds over time. Confirm whether your degree qualifies — your DSO can tell you.

## The 90-day unemployment rule

During OPT you can only be unemployed for 90 days total (150 days with STEM extension). Days accumulate whether you're tracking them or not.

### What counts as employment

- ✅ Full-time job
- ✅ Part-time job (must be related to your degree)
- ✅ Contract / freelance work (if structured correctly — talk to your DSO)
- ❌ Volunteer work
- Unpaid internships may qualify if structured correctly — confirm with your DSO
- ❌ Job searching
> ⚠️ Track your days. Your DSO tracks them too. If you're approaching 60 days unemployed, escalate your urgency.

## Finding companies that actually sponsor

> 💡 Don’t apply randomly. Start with companies that have already sponsored your role. History predicts sponsorship better than anything a job description says. Many companies say “we consider all candidates” and mean “we don’t sponsor.” The H1B filing record is the truth.

Go to H1Bdata.info and search by job title — not just company name. Look for:

- Companies that have filed H1B petitions for your exact job title
- Volume of filings over the past 3 years (more = established process)
- Approval rates
| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| 100+ petitions, 90%+ approval rate | Established sponsor, real process |
| 10–50 petitions, good approval rate | Sponsors selectively — ask about process early |
| 1–5 petitions total | Case-by-case — high risk |
| Zero petitions | Almost certainly won't sponsor despite what they say |

### General patterns (not guarantees — always verify)

- ✅ Typically sponsor: Large tech (FAANG, Microsoft), Big 4 consulting, major banks, large healthcare/pharma
- ⚠️ Varies: Startups, mid-size tech, consulting boutiques
- ❌ Rarely/never: Government contractors, small businesses, most retail/hospitality

## When to bring up visa status

Bring it up before the final round — not at the offer stage. Raising it after 4 rounds of interviews wastes everyone's time.

Best time: the recruiter screen, or right after you pass it.

> "I want to be transparent — I'm currently on F-1 OPT and will need H1B sponsorship down the line. I wanted to make sure that's something [Company] is able to support before we go further."

## The H1B lottery

| Date | What happens |
|---|---|
| ~Early March | Registration window opens |
| ~Late March | Lottery results announced |
| April–June | Selected employers file full petitions |
| October 1 | H1B status begins (new fiscal year) |

- 65,000 regular cap
- 20,000 additional for US master's degree holders — two lottery entries if you have a US master's
> ⚠️ Lottery odds vary year to year — check the USCIS website for the most recent selection rates. Even doing everything right, there’s a real chance of not being selected the first time. Plan for this. Have a backup plan.

## Cap-gap extension

If your employer files an H-1B petition that is selected in the lottery, your OPT automatically extends until October 1. This bridges the gap between OPT expiration and your H-1B start date.

> ⚠️ Cap-gap only applies if the H-1B petition requests a change of status. If your employer filed for consular processing instead, cap-gap does not apply. Confirm with your DSO which type was filed.

Source: [USCIS Cap-Gap Extension](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/students/cap-gap-extension)

## OPT job search checklist

### ☐ Before graduation

☐ OPT application submitted to DSO (60+ days before graduation)

☐ Confirmed whether your degree is STEM-designated

☐ Job search actively started

### ☐ Once OPT begins

☐ Tracking unemployment days

☐ Every company checked on H1Bdata.info before applying

☐ Visa status raised in recruiter screen, not at offer stage

### ☐ At offer stage

☐ Confirmed company is E-Verify enrolled

☐ Sponsorship commitment discussed with HR

☐ Understand start date relative to H1B filing timeline

### ☐ H1B cycle

☐ Employer registers by early March (confirm dates on uscis.gov)

☐ Know lottery result before making other plans

☐ Have a backup plan if not selected
