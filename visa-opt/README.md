# Visa / OPT Track

## Read this before you start applying

If you're on F-1 OPT or about to be, your job search has constraints that don't apply to other candidates. Ignoring them doesn't make them go away — it creates problems later.

This section is not about immigration law. It's about how to run your job search given the reality of your situation.

---

## The OPT timeline — what you can't miss

```
60 days before graduation    Apply for OPT through your DSO (international student office)
                             USCIS processing takes 3-5 months — apply early

Graduation                   OPT start date can be up to 60 days after graduation
                             You choose the start date — pick strategically

OPT active                   12 months to work (24-month STEM extension available)
                             90-day unemployment limit — days without a job count

Before OPT expires           Employer files H1B (April lottery, starts October)
                             STEM OPT extension buys additional time if not selected
```

**The mistake most people make:** Waiting until graduation to start the job search. By then you may have already burned 60 days of your unemployment clock. Start your search before you graduate.

---

## STEM OPT extension

If your degree is in a STEM field (most CS, data, engineering, many business analytics programs), you're eligible for a 24-month OPT extension — giving you 36 months total.

**Requirements:**
- Degree must be on the STEM designated degree program list (check uscis.gov)
- Employer must be enrolled in E-Verify
- Must apply before your initial OPT expires

This matters for your job search: STEM OPT gives you two more H1B lottery cycles. That dramatically improves your odds. Confirm whether your degree qualifies — your DSO can tell you.

---

## The 90-day unemployment rule

During OPT you can only be unemployed for 90 days total (150 days with STEM extension). Days accumulate whether you're tracking them or not.

**Practically:**
- Start your job search before graduation
- If you're approaching 60 days unemployed, escalate urgency
- Part-time work and contract work count — some people do this while searching
- Volunteer work and unpaid internships do NOT count toward employment

Track your days. Your DSO tracks them too.

## Finding companies that actually sponsor

Many companies say "we consider all candidates" and mean "we don't sponsor."

**How to vet companies before you apply:**

1. Go to **H1Bdata.info**
2. Search the company name
3. Look at: how many H1B petitions filed, for what roles, at what salaries, and approval rates

A company with 200 filings and 180 approvals over 3 years — real sponsor.
A company with zero filings claiming they "consider sponsorship" — probably not worth your time.

See [sponsorship.md](./sponsorship.md) for a detailed guide on vetting companies.

## When to bring up visa status

Bring it up before the final round, not at the offer stage.

Raising it at the offer stage after 4 rounds of interviews wastes everyone's time and burns goodwill.

A good time: the recruiter screen, or right after you pass it.

> *"I want to be transparent — I'm currently on F-1 OPT and will need H1B sponsorship down the line. I wanted to make sure that's something [Company] is able to support before we go further."*

Most recruiters will appreciate the directness. If they can't sponsor, you've saved yourself weeks.

## The H1B lottery — what to expect

Your employer files an H1B petition on your behalf in April. USCIS runs a lottery.

```
April 1        Registration window opens
Mid-April      Lottery results announced
April–June     Selected employers file full petitions
October 1      H1B status begins (new fiscal year)
```

**Cap numbers (approximate):**
- 65,000 regular cap
- 20,000 additional for US master's degree holders — if you have a US master's, you get two lottery entries

Lottery odds have been roughly 30-40% in recent years. Even doing everything right, there's a real chance of not being selected the first time. Plan for this. Have a backup plan.

## CPT — if you're still in school

Curricular Practical Training lets you work while enrolled.

**Key things:**
- Must be authorized by your DSO
- Must be directly related to your degree
- Full-time CPT for 12+ months eliminates your OPT eligibility
- Part-time CPT does not affect OPT

If you're using CPT for an internship: confirm it doesn't accidentally eat your OPT. Talk to your DSO before accepting any CPT authorization.

## If your job search is running long

**Short term:**
- Freelance / contract work — counts as employment if structured correctly, talk to your DSO
- Part-time role — buys time while you search full-time
- On-campus employment — limited but an option if still connected to university

**Medium term:**
- STEM OPT extension — apply before your OPT expires if eligible
- Graduate school — resets your F-1 clock, gives you another OPT cycle
- Change of status — talk to an immigration attorney, not Reddit

**Important:** If your OPT expires and you haven't filed for an extension or changed status, you fall out of status immediately. There is no grace period. Talk to your DSO well before that date.

## The emotional reality

OPT job search is harder than regular job search. You're doing everything everyone else is doing, plus filtering out companies that won't sponsor, having awkward visa conversations, working against a hard deadline, and watching peers get offers from companies that won't look at you.

That's real and it's unfair.

The candidates who navigate OPT well start early, are upfront about their status, and focus energy on companies with real sponsorship history — rather than applying broadly and hoping.

## OPT job search checklist

```
Before graduation
□ OPT application submitted to DSO (60+ days before graduation)
□ Confirmed whether your degree is STEM-designated
□ Job search actively started

Once OPT begins
□ Tracking your unemployment days
□ Every company checked on H1Bdata.info before applying
□ Visa status raised in recruiter screen, not at offer stage

At offer stage
□ Confirmed company is E-Verify enrolled (required for STEM extension)
□ Sponsorship commitment discussed
□ Understand start date relative to H1B filing timeline

H1B cycle
□ Employer registers by April 1
□ Know your lottery result before making other plans
□ Have a backup plan if not selected
```
